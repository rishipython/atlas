### Cross‑correlation

`scipy.signal.correlate2d` can do exactly what I need – it multiplies one image with every possible offset of the other and sums the products (`mode="full"` gives me every possible displacement).  
The `method="fft"` flag forces an FFT‑based implementation which scales like `O(N log N)` instead of `O(N^3)` – perfect for larger images without any Python loops at all:

```python
corr = correlate2d(
    A.astype(np.float64),
    B.astype(np.float64),
    mode="full",
    boundary="fill",
    fillvalue=0,
    method="fft",
)
```

I cast explicitly to `float64` so even integer inputs get promoted safely – no overflow worries even when the images are big integers or have many pixels set high.
